What I Learned at LISA 2016 Conference

December 18, 2016 · 1 min · Aleksey Tsalolikhin

Attended LISA 2016 in Boston, exhibiting and promoting training services. Strong interest in Machine Learning and Go programming instruction. Shared a success story from “Time Management for System Administrators” training at Ohio Linux Fest.

At the Training Sysadmins Birds of a Feather (BoF) session, learned about Adobe’s internal training culture. Received a LOPSA challenge coin for running the Los Angeles chapter.

Top Three Learnings#

  1. Jupyter Notebook — discovered through a Machine Learning presentation
  2. Unikernels — emerging technology discussion
  3. SRE Practices — closing keynote “SRE in the Small and in the Large” showed SRE benefits organizations of all sizes

Jane Adams’ keynote on emergence in complex systems was also highlighted as especially memorable.
