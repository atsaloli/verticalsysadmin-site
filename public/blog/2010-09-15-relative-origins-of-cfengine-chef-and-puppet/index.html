Relative Origin of Cfengine, Puppet and Chef

September 15, 2010 · 1 min · Aleksey Tsalolikhin

Cfengine: Pioneered configuration description languages for Unix, introduced “classes” for conditional logic. Version 2 added convergence and self-healing.

Puppet: Luke Kanies, a power Cfengine 2 user, created it to address his frustrations. “Graph-based and model-driven” with a “simple DSL language (designed to be simple, safe and human readable)” plus Ruby extensions.

Chef: Adam Jacobs, dissatisfied with Puppet’s non-deterministic default execution ordering, built Chef with “tightly ordered” sequence control. Innovative cloud provisioning for VM instantiation and automated configuration. Originated in the Ruby-on-Rails cloud ecosystem.

Cfengine 3: Complete rewrite of version 2, simplified syntax, introduced Promise Theory, added Knowledge Management and Business Integration features, database and Windows registry management.
