A self-paced curriculum for preparing entry-level professionals for Linux sysadmin roles. Compiled with assistance from [Mike Weilgart](https://github.com/mikeweilgart).

## Part I – Linux Essentials

- Text: ["Linux Essentials"](http://www.amazon.com/Linux-Essentials-Christine-Bresnahan/dp/111909206X) (2015)
- Online: [Introduction to Linux (LFS101x) via edX](https://www.edx.org/course/introduction-linux-linuxfoundationx-lfs101x-2)
- Investment: 40–60 hours, ~$26

## Part II – The Linux Command Line

- [Free PDF](http://downloads.sourceforge.net/project/linuxcommand/TLCL/13.07/TLCL-13.07.pdf), 537 pages

## Part III – Linux System Administration

- [Linux Foundation's Essentials course (LFS201)](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/linux-courses/system-administration-training/essentials-of-system-administration), includes DNS training
- 40–50 hours, $499 (certification included)

## Part IV – Certification (Optional)

- [LPIC-1](https://www.lpi.org/certification/get-certified-lpi/lpic-1-linux-server-professional/) or [Linux Foundation LFCS](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/lfcs)

## Part V – Computer Science Fundamentals

- [Harvard's CS50x via edX](https://www.edx.org/course/introduction-computer-science-harvardx-cs50x) (can begin after employment)

Total cost: ~$1,030. Time: 2–4 months full-time or 6–12 months part-time. Prerequisites: Basic computer literacy.
