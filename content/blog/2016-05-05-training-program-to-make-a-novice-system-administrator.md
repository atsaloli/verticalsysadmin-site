date: 2016-05-05

I've put together a program to make entry-level Linux [system administrators](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_administrator) because we need more to maintain the IT fabric of modern civilization.

The program is self-paced, using online resources.  This is not training I deliver personally, rather I will guide you to quality training resources, in the correct sequence, and work with you to debug any stops. There is no fee to me for this program. The training takes 2-3 months full-time and costs about USD 1,000 for the online training courses and books. You'll need a practice computer you can install Linux on.

Pre-requisites: Aspirants need to know how to use a computer for basic tasks (e.g., Web, email, word processing).

**Part I - Linux Essentials**

Read ["Linux Essentials"](http://www.amazon.com/Linux-Essentials-Christine-Bresnahan/dp/111909206X) \[2015\]

342 pages

$25.80

Do the ["Introduction to Linux" (LFS101x)](https://www.edx.org/course/introduction-linux-linuxfoundationx-lfs101x-2) \[2014\]

Online course

40-60 hours Free

**Part II - The Linux Command Line**

Read ["The Linux Command Line"](http://downloads.sourceforge.net/project/linuxcommand/TLCL/13.07/TLCL-13.07.pdf) \[2013\]

537 pages

Free

**Part III - Linux System Administration**

Do the ["Essentials of System Administration" (LFS201)](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/linux-courses/system-administration-training/essentials-of-system-administration)

Online course 40-50 hours $499 (includes certification exam)

Do Cricket Liu's DNS course from the [Linux Sysadmin Basics](http://shop.oreilly.com/category/learning-path/linux-system-administration-basics.do)

Video training from oreilly.com

Free with a 10 day Safari trial

 **Part IV - Certification (optional)** 

\- [Linux Professional Institute Linux Server Professional (LPIC-1)](https://www.lpi.org/certification/get-certified-lpi/lpic-1-linux-server-professional/)  $42.49 at Pearson VUE

\- [Linux Foundation Certified System Administrator (LFCS)](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/lfcs) included with LFS201

 **Part V - Introduction to Computer Science (can be done after starting work)** 

[Introduction to Computer Science](https://www.edx.org/course/introduction-computer-science-harvardx-cs50x) (CS50x) online training from edX.org 100 - 200 hours

Free

**Tips for Success**

[Mike Weilgart](https://github.com/mikeweilgart) completed the program and adds:

And a crucial ingredient to success (and I'm being entirely serious): Spend **plenty** of time during your training reading up on what you're studying on the [Unix & Linux Stack Exchange](http://unix.stackexchange.com).

**Elective - Learn How to Make a Web Site**

Exercise: Setup a personal site, e.g., [like Mark Burgess has](http://markburgess.org/)

There is an excellent WordPress tutorial at [http://websitesetup.org/](http://websitesetup.org)

To learn the underlying technology, HTML/CSS, I recommend [http://websitesetup.org/html-beginners-guide/](http://websitesetup.org/html-beginners-guide/)

Also, you can write in [Markdown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markdown) and use [pandoc](http://pandoc.org/) to convert to HTML. Try it!

**Elective - Learn How to Use strace to Spy on Your Programs**

http://jvns.ca/strace-zine-unfolded.pdf

**Elective - Postgres Database Administration**

"The Accidental DBA" (or, Administering PostgreSQL When It's Not Your Job) to those just getting into Postgres database server administration. Josh Berkus, from the PostgreSQL Core Team, presented it in 2015 (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qoQRGl\_P2aQ (2.5 hour video)) and the original (2012) slides by Christophe Pettus at http://thebuild.com/blog/2012/06/04/postgresql-when-its-not-your-job-at-djangocon-europe/
