USENIX Annual Technical Conference and LISA are the first professional conferences I went to as a fledgeling sysadmin. USENIX will always hold a special place in my heart. I attribute my professional success to regular training at USENIX conferences.

I really enjoyed LISA '16 in Boston.

This year I exhibited, promoting our [training services](http://www.verticalsysadmin.com/). I learned that people are looking for training on Machine Learning (Big Data) and on Go; and I got to show Tom Limoncelli a success story from my ["Time Management for System Administrators"](http://www.verticalsysadmin.com/tm4sa.htm) training at [Ohio Linux Fest](https://ohiolinux.org/) that I had received just that morning.

At the Training Sysadmins BoF, I learned Adobe has a kick-ass in-house training program that keeps getting better. Really impressed with [Adobe culture](http://www.forbes.com/sites/davidburkus/2016/06/01/how-adobe-scrapped-its-performance-review-system-and-why-it-worked/)!

It was revitalizing to hear the passion for improving society at the Educating Sysadmins BoF. Keep up the great work!

At the LOPSA Annual Community Meeting, I was awarded a LOPSA [challenge coin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Challenge_coin) in recognition for running the [Los Angeles chapter](http://www.meetup.com/lopsala) and signing up new members. I love it! Thank you!

![](https://verticalsysadmin.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/12/atsaloli_accepting_challenge_coin.jpeg?w=300&h=300)

[Nick Anderson](https://twitter.com/@cmdln_) asked what are the top three things I learned at LISA.

First, I just want to say "kudos!" to the Program Committee - lots of great (and very modern) content!

- I learned about the [Jupyter Notebook](http://jupyter.org/) programming and data visualization environment in the class on [Machine Learning](https://www.usenix.org/conference/lisa16/conference-program/presentation/harrison).
- I loved the talk on [unikernels](https://www.usenix.org/conference/lisa16/conference-program/presentation/levine). Technology keeps changing and LISA helps me keep up.
- The closing session, "SRE in the Small and in the Large" demonstrated SRE is not just for Google scale -- even smaller organizations can reap large benefits from applying "a stitch in times saves nine". Read the [book](http://shop.oreilly.com/product/0636920041528.do)!

Nick, the keynote by Jane Adams on [emergence in complex systems](https://www.usenix.org/conference/lisa16/conference-program/presentation/adams) was mindblowing -- look for it online soon! (Here is a [shorter one](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D9LiMrcm7Kg), from three years ago.)

My favorite part of the conference was hanging out with my LISA friends. Hope to see you [in October in San Francisco](https://www.usenix.org/conference/lisa17)!
